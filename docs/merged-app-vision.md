# Poker Ledger — Vision

## Who it's for

You host a Wednesday cash game at your place and play a Saturday one at your friend's. Sometimes you track every buy-in live on your phone as it happens. Other times nobody's tracking until the last hand — and the only record is a phone screenshot of everyone's final chip stacks, scribbled on the house rules app. You want one place that handles both, remembers you across both, and tells you how you're actually doing over a year of poker — not just this one night.

Your co-star is the host. They're usually a player too, but they're the one getting texted "wait, did Ankit pay me?" three days after the fact. They need the ledger to be right, to be fast, and to not require everyone at the table to download an app.

## What it is

**Poker Ledger** is a home-game ledger with two modes under one roof:

- **Live mode** — Everyone at the table signs in on their phone. Buy-ins, rebuys, and cashouts are logged as they happen. When the game ends, settlements are already calculated — no math, no arguments.
- **Quick-log mode** *(what we used to call "Fishes")* — No phones at the table. At the end of the night, you snap a photo of the final tally, drop it in the app, and it reads the numbers, matches the names, and writes the session to your ledger. Edit anything that's off, confirm, done.

Both modes write to the **same ledger**. Your name, your history, your running P/L — one of each.

## The thing the merge unlocks

**One lifetime number across every game you've played, regardless of how it was tracked.**

That's the value prop. Not "one login" (nice, but table-stakes). Not "cross-mode winrate splits" (a fun stat, but a feature, not a reason to exist). The reason to exist is: **the app finally knows you're the same player at the Wednesday game and the Saturday game, and tells you the truth about your year.**

Every other benefit — shared settlement history, one profile, cross-mode leaderboards among your regular crew — flows out of that single fact: one person, one ledger, many ways to record a night.

## What it doesn't do

- **It doesn't merge groups for you.** If your Wednesday crew and your Saturday crew are separate, the app keeps them separate. You, as a player, are unified. The games aren't.
- **It doesn't track hands or cards.** We log money in, money out, and when. That's it. No hand histories, no coaching, no "you should've folded."
- **It doesn't require everyone to use Live mode.** A lot of tables will never adopt it, and that's fine — Quick-log covers them. Neither mode is second-class.
- **It isn't a Venmo replacement.** We show you who owes who and deep-link to the payment app of your choice. We don't move money.
- **It isn't a casino / online-poker tool.** Home games only. That's the whole audience.

## A note on the name

I'm calling it **Poker Ledger**. It says what it is, nobody has to ask twice, and it survives us adding a third mode someday. The old names ("Thor", "Fishes") were internal codenames and should be retired everywhere users can see them — menus, titles, URLs, the login screen, all of it.
